## 0. Locked Parameters (inherited, do not change)

- **Genre:** Dark-fantasy survival-RPG.
- **Platform / scope:** PC + Console, Steam-first. Single-player **and** small-group co-op (2–4). Indie-to-mid budget.
- **Tone:** Dark fantasy — a cursed, decaying world where magic, craft, and survival are entangled.

## 1. Working Title + High Concept

**Working title:** **WITHERREACH**

**One-line high concept (elevator pitch):**
*A survival-RPG in a world that is actively rotting to death, where the corruption killing the land is also the only source of your power — every meal, every spell, every level pulls you closer to becoming the thing you're fighting.*

**3-line pitch (for the executive summary):**
> The Witherfall ended the world; now the land lingers in an endless rotting dusk that will not let anything truly die. You are a Revenant — one of the unburied dead — who must scavenge power from the very Blight that is eating creation, hold a fragile hearth-light against the dark, and decide how much of your humanity you'll burn to push the death of the world back by one more day.
> Power and survival are the **same meter** pulling opposite ways: bank corruption to grow strong, or purge it to stay human and safe — you cannot do both.
> Solo or with 2–3 others, you race a world that is dying with or without you toward the source of the rot, and choose to end it, master it, or be consumed by it.

---

## 2. Player Fantasy / Core Experience

You are a **Revenant**: something that should be dead and isn't, kept walking by the same curse that broke the world. You are not a chosen hero. You are a scavenger of the apocalypse's aftermath, picking power out of poison.

What the player should **feel**, in order of priority:
1. **Tense scarcity under a closing dark** — light is fuel, food is a gamble, safety is something you build and lose.
2. **The seduction of power that costs you yourself** — every upgrade is also a temptation; getting stronger visibly, mechanically corrupts you.
3. **Hard-won mastery** — deliberate, weighty combat and deep build-craft that rewards understanding the corruption economy.
4. **Melancholic awe** — the world is beautiful in its decay; pushing back the dark, even briefly, feels earned and fragile.
5. **In co-op: desperate interdependence** — survival forces specialization; you literally bleed power into each other to stay alive.

---

## 3. Design Pillars (LOCKED — 5)

1. **One Meter, Two Masters.** Corruption is simultaneously your power source and your survival threat; every meaningful choice trades safety for strength or strength for safety. *(This is the keystone — see §8.)*
2. **The World Is Already Dying.** The environment is an active, decaying antagonist on a clock; standing still loses ground. You hold back collapse, you never finally win it.
3. **Power Has a Body Count.** Character growth visibly marks, warps, and endangers you — the strongest builds live closest to death and to "turning." Build identity *is* corruption identity.
4. **Earn the Light.** Safety is constructed and maintained, never granted. Every Hearth is a held breath against the dark; the map is a tide you push back hearth by hearth.
5. **Die Forward.** Death feeds the world and your own hollowing; you lose ground and resources, not the story. Permadeath is a slow descent you fight, not a sudden wipe.

## 4. Unique Selling Points + Differentiation

### Top USPs
1. **Corruption-as-currency (the unified meter).** Survival pressure and RPG power are *one resource* (the Blight, carried as **Taint**) pulling in opposite directions. There are no orthogonal "chore meters" — eating, spellcasting, crafting, and leveling all transact in the same corruption economy. This is the core innovation and the thing nothing else on the market does cleanly.
2. **A world dying on a clock, with or without you (the Long Dusk).** The land actively decays and encroaches over time. Reclaiming territory (kindling Greater Hearths) is real but **impermanent** unless maintained — the opposite of the static conquerable sandbox.
3. **Identity = your relationship to the rot (Warded vs Tainted paths).** Your build literally sets your survival difficulty. The strongest, most magical builds run the hottest Taint and live nearest to "turning." Co-op parties self-organize around this (a Warded anchor sheltering Tainted strikers).
4. **Mechanical soft-permadeath: Hollowing & turning.** Death and overexposure advance a permanent corruption track; max it and your character *turns* into a Wake-creature the world (and other players) may later face. Stakes without roguelike erasure.
5. **Blight-transfer revival.** Co-op revive is a literal sacrifice — you pour your banked power into a downed ally to stabilize them, trading your strength for their life.

### Explicit "we are X but Y" differentiation
- **vs Valheim:** We are Valheim's build-craft-and-boss-progression loop, **but** the world is rotting on a clock and your power comes from the same corruption that's killing you; reclaiming land is a desperate, impermanent push rather than a permanent victory.
- **vs V Rising:** We are V Rising's dark survival-ARPG fusion, **but** single-player-first PvE-focused, with the "blood/feeding" tension generalized into a full corruption economy that governs your entire build and survival risk (no PvP-server dependency).
- **vs Dark Souls / Soulslikes:** We are a Soulslike's weighty combat, bonfire rhythm, and hollowing dread, **but** embedded in a persistent survival world — the bonfire is a Hearth you build and defend, the "souls" are literally your survival fuel, and hollowing is a long mechanical descent you co-op against.
- **vs Don't Starve:** We share the funereal dread and light-vs-dark survival loop **but** with real RPG build depth, 3D weighty combat, and a structured narrative arc instead of roguelike runs.
- **vs Conan Exiles / Project Zomboid:** We share hardcore consequence and deep systems **but** trade brutal open-PvP sandboxing / pure simulation for a curated dark-fantasy RPG with a central corruption mechanic and a story to finish.

## 5. Setting & World Premise

**The cataclysm — the Witherfall.** Generations ago, the living world tried to cheat death. In a rite called **the Communion**, its people bound their souls into the land itself to become immortal. It worked wrong. Now the land cannot let anything truly die — but it cannot keep anything alive either. The sun guttered out into the earth; the world fell into a permanent rotting twilight (the **Long Dusk**). Everything lingers: corpses walk, ruins fester, and a corrupting substance — the **Blight** — bleeds from every wound in creation.

**The place — the Reach.** The playable world is the **Witherreach**, the drowned-in-rot remains of a dead kingdom. It is divided into regions that exist in escalating *states of decay*; the Blight tide creeps inward over time, reclaiming what isn't actively held by a lit Hearth.

**Why you must survive.** You are a **Revenant** — a soul that the Communion snagged but could not bind, dragged back into a half-corpse. You are not safe and you are not done: the longer you exist, the more the Blight works into you (Hollowing). You survive because the alternative isn't peace — it's *turning* into one of the mindless, ravenous **Wake**. To stop existing on the world's terms, you must reach the wound at the world's heart.

**The central hook / mystery.** Something at the center of the Reach — the **Hollow Crown**, the dead god-king who led the Communion and is now the rotting engine of the Long Dusk — is the source of the Blight. The mystery the player unravels: *Why won't the world die? Can it be allowed to? And what are you, really, that you can walk in the rot and bend it?* The endgame is a choice at the Hollow Crown (see §9).

**Factions (locked, minimal — for narrative writers to expand):**
- **The Ashen Wardens** — survivors who resist corruption: keepers of Hearths, lightcraft, and the old human ways. Allied to the **Warded** path. Distrust the Tainted.
- **The Hollowing / the Communed** — those who *embraced* the Blight to grow strong; some lucid, most halfway to the Wake. Allied to the **Tainted** path. Believe mastery, not purity, is salvation.
- **The Wake** — the lost: corrupted dead and beasts, the world's ambient threat (see §10). Not a faction with intent; a rising tide.

## 6. Tone, Mood & References

**Tone:** Oppressive, melancholic, and beautiful-in-decay. Folk-horror crossed with funereal grandeur. Hope exists but is a verb — something you work for and can lose. Grimdark, not nihilistic: the point of the dark is to make the light cost something.

**Mood targets:** dread under scarcity; quiet awe at a rotting-gorgeous world; the creeping unease of watching yourself change.

**Reference touchstones (for art, narrative, audio alignment):**
- **Games:** Bloodborne / Dark Souls (gothic decay, weighty combat, hollowing), Don't Starve (light-vs-dark survival dread, silhouette horror — but grimmer and grounded), Sunless Sea (textual dread, a hostile dark), Diablo (oppressive gloom), Darkwood (folk-horror survival).
- **Film:** *Annihilation* (the Shimmer = the Blight's seductive, alien beauty/horror), *The VVitch* (folk-horror austerity), *Princess Mononoke* (rot-gods, nature corrupted), *The Road* (survival bleakness and tenderness).
- **Books:** Gene Wolfe, *The Book of the New Sun* (dying-earth, the Long Dusk register), Jeff VanderMeer, *Annihilation* (corruption as transformation), Mark Lawrence (grimdark voice), Cormac McCarthy, *The Road* (scarcity, moral weight).
- **Audio direction:** sparse low drones, funeral folk instrumentation (cello, bowed metal, ritual percussion), long silences broken by the dark's "breath."

## 7. Core Gameplay Loop

### Moment-to-moment (seconds–minutes)
Explore the gloom with a finite light radius (light suppresses Taint gain; dark accelerates it). Read the environment for resource nodes (clean vs blighted), threats (the Wake), and decay state. Engage in **deliberate stamina-based combat** (melee / ranged / rot-magic — see §9) or avoid it. Every action transacts in the corruption economy: eat tainted food to fend off hunger but raise Taint; spend Taint to cast or empower; manage your light fuel.

### Per-session — an **Expedition** (30–90 min)
Leave your **Hearth**, push outward into more-decayed and Blight-encroached zones, gather resources and advance toward an objective (a **Reliquary** landmark or a resource frontier). Survive the round trip before your Taint, light, supplies, or nerve run out. Return to the Hearth to **bank** Taint (keep it as power potential) or **purge** it (spend the safety, lose the power), repair/upgrade gear, improve the Hearth, and advance your path. The bank-or-purge decision is the session's climactic choice.

### Long-term / meta-progression (tens of hours)
- **Reclaim the map:** defeat region **Wardens** (bosses) to kindle **Greater Hearths**, rolling back the Long Dusk locally — but decay creeps back unless maintained, so the map is a tide, not a checklist.
- **Ascend a path:** invest along **Warded** (resist) and/or **Tainted** (embrace) trees plus martial/craft skills; skills *ascend* by spending Blight, tightening the link between build power and corruption load.
- **Race the clock:** the global **Long Dusk** deepens through **Tides** (eras of escalating rot and tougher Wake), pressuring the player to progress.
- **End the story:** reach the **Hollow Crown** and choose the ending (see §9). Settlement and world state persist across sessions.

## 8. The RPG ↔ Survival Fusion Thesis (THE KEYSTONE — design risk, resolved)

**Thesis (locked):** *Survival pressure and character power are the same resource, the Blight, expressed through one carried meter (**Taint**) that the player must constantly trade in two opposite directions. Survival meters are not separate chores bolted onto an RPG — they ARE the RPG economy.*

This resolves the genre's classic failure mode (survival meters feel like orthogonal busywork; RPG growth feels like a separate power-fantasy treadmill) by making them one loop.

### The three corruption quantities (single source of truth — all downstream docs use these terms)
1. **Taint** — your *current carried corruption*. It is **both** (a) spendable currency for casting rot-magic, tempering gear, and ascending skills, **and** (b) your primary survival-threat meter. It rises from surviving the corrupted world and falls only by purging at a Hearth.
2. **Hollowing** — *permanent accumulated corruption*. Advances on death and from sustained max-Taint exposure. Cannot be purged by normal means; it is the soft-permadeath track. At maximum, your character **turns** (see §11).
3. **Blight (resource)** — the world-side substance: blighted nodes, tainted food, Wake essence. Harvesting/consuming Blight converts into Taint. This is the *supply* feeding the meter.

### How they interlock (the loop, mechanically)
- **Survival → power:** To not starve and not freeze in the dark, you must engage the corrupted world (clean food is rare and weak; blighted food is plentiful and nourishing but raises Taint; darkness raises Taint). Surviving therefore *generates* the very Taint you spend to grow.
- **Power → risk:** Spending Taint makes you strong, but **carrying** high Taint to spend later is dangerous: it accelerates wound festering, rots your carried food/supplies faster, mutates you, and — critically — **wakes the Wake** (high Taint increases hunt pressure; predators are drawn to corruption). So banking power literally raises the difficulty in real time.
- **The recurring decision:** At the Hearth, **bank** (keep Taint as power potential, accept ongoing survival danger) or **purge** (convert to safety, forfeit the power you'd banked). Neither is correct; it's the core tension every session.
- **Build = survival difficulty:** Your path and slotted skills set your *baseline Taint floor*. A **Tainted** glass-cannon build (rot-magic, mutations) runs a permanently high Taint floor — devastating but living one bad expedition from turning. A **Warded** build keeps a low floor — stable, lower ceiling, the party's anchor. Choosing a build *is* choosing your survival mode.
- **Light, warmth, shelter — folded in, not bolted on:** Darkness is the world's corrupting breath → light/fire suppresses Taint gain (so "warmth/shelter" pressure is really *Taint-rate management*). Hunger forces engagement with the Blight food economy (so "hunger" is really the *Taint supply on-ramp*). There are **no standalone temperature/sanity bars** — those pressures are expressed through the one Taint economy. (Hunger remains a real, visible need, but its only sustainable answer routes through Blight.)

### Why this is safe to build (risk mitigation, for systems writers)
- It collapses N meters into 1 economy → fewer competing systems to balance, one clear tuning axis.
- It guarantees survival actions are always also build-relevant → no "dead" survival busywork.
- It gives co-op a built-in role structure (Warded anchor + Tainted strikers + Blight-transfer support).
- **Tuning guardrails (locked intent, numbers TBD by systems experts):** purge must always be *available but costly*; Taint must be *spendable faster than it floors* so players aren't trapped; turning must be *telegraphed and stave-off-able*, never a surprise wipe.

## 9. Endgame & Player Choice (locked frame)

At the **Hollow Crown**, the player chooses one of three endings (narrative writers expand; the frame is fixed):
1. **End it (the Pyre):** unmake the Communion — let the world finally die, including yourself. The "merciful" ending.
2. **Master it (the Crown):** seize the engine of the Blight; become the new sovereign of the rot. The "power" ending (favored by deep Tainted builds).
3. **Be consumed (the Hollowing):** fail or surrender — full turning. The "tragic/loss" ending, also reachable by maxing Hollowing in normal play.

## 10. Combat, Enemies & AI (locked frame)

- **Combat feel:** deliberate, stamina-gated, weighty (Soulslike weight, not action-RPG button-mash). Three intertwined modes: **melee** (stamina + positioning), **ranged** (scarce ammo, scavenged), **rot-magic** (powered by spending Taint — the bridge between combat and the corruption economy).
- **The Wake (ambient antagonist):** corrupted dead and beasts; the world's rising tide. Spawn pressure scales with the player's **Taint** (corruption draws the hunt) and with the **Long Dusk** Tide. They are dread and attrition, not just XP piñatas.
- **Wardens (bosses):** region-guardian set-pieces gating Greater Hearths and progression. Each Warden = a biome's apex corruption.
- **Turned players/NPCs:** a character who fully Hollows becomes a Wake-creature that can populate the world later (emergent, co-op-relevant).

## 11. Death Model & Session Structure (LOCKED)

**Not roguelike permadeath. Not consequence-free respawn.** A two-tier model that feeds back into the corruption economy:

- **On death:** respawn at your last lit **Hearth** (bonfire-style). You **drop your banked Taint** as a recoverable corpse-cache (Souls-style corpse-run — retrieve it or lose the power potential). Each death advances your permanent **Hollowing** by a step.
- **Soft permadeath — turning:** when **Hollowing** reaches maximum, the character **turns** into a Wake-creature (a real, telegraphed, stave-off-able descent — not a surprise). This is the survival-RPG's "real" death, and you fight it for the whole game.
- **Co-op death:** a downed player can be **revived within a window** by an ally performing a **Blight-transfer** (the ally sacrifices banked Taint to stabilize them). If not revived in time, the downed player respawns at the shared Hearth under the corpse-run rules above.
- **Session structure:** persistent world and settlement; play is organized as **Expeditions** out from and back to the Hearth (see §7), under the deepening **Long Dusk** clock.

## 12. Target Audience + Closest Comparables

**Primary audience:** PC/console players who love deep survival-crafting (Valheim, V Rising) **and** want genuine RPG build depth, weighty Soulslike combat, and a grimdark narrative — players comfortable with difficulty, scarcity, and consequence. Core co-op unit: 2–4 friends. Age 18–35 core. Secondary: solo dark-fantasy RPG/Soulslike fans.

**Three closest comparables ("we are X but Y"):**
1. **Valheim** — *but the world rots on a clock and your power is the corruption killing it; reclaiming land is impermanent.*
2. **V Rising** — *but single-player-first PvE, with feeding/blood generalized into a full corruption economy that drives your whole build.*
3. **Dark Souls (Soulslike)** — *but in a persistent survival world: the bonfire is a Hearth you build and defend, souls are your survival fuel, hollowing is a mechanical descent you co-op against.*

## 14. Shared Glossary (canonical terms — all writers use these verbatim)

- **Witherreach / the Reach** — the playable dead-kingdom world.
- **the Witherfall** — the cataclysm (a failed immortality rite, the **Communion**) that broke the world.
- **the Long Dusk** — the permanent rotting twilight; also the global decay clock that deepens in **Tides**.
- **the Blight** — the corrupting substance bleeding from the world; the world-side resource feeding corruption.
- **Taint** — current carried corruption: spendable power currency AND primary survival-threat meter.
- **Hollowing** — permanent accumulated corruption; the soft-permadeath track; maxing it = **turning**.
- **turning** — a character fully consumed by Hollowing becomes a **Wake**-creature.

- **Reliquaries** — landmark objectives / dungeons in the wilds.
- **Paths: the Warded** (resist corruption — stable, lower ceiling, anchor/support) vs **the Tainted** (embrace corruption — high power, high instability, lives near turning).
- **the Hollow Crown** — the dead god-king at the world's heart; source of the Blight; site of the endgame choice (**End it / Master it / Be consumed**).
