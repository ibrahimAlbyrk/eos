## 1. The Resolved Mystery (THE SPINE)

The bible poses three questions (§5): *Why won't the world die? Can it be allowed to? What are you, really, that you can walk in the rot and bend it?* Here is the binding answer. Everything downstream — factions, endings, regions — is a facet of this.

**Why won't the world die.**
The Communion was a soul-binding. The Choir [proposed canon] sang every soul of the living kingdom — people, beasts, the land itself — into a single shared web, anchored to one living vessel at the centre: the king. The promise was *renewal*: when a body failed, its soul would return to the web and be re-clothed in new flesh. Immortality as an unbroken cycle.

It worked for one breath. Then the anchor — the king's mortal body, already old (that is *why* he feared death and reached for the rite) — died. With no living vessel at the centre, **the web locked**. Souls can no longer leave (the binding holds them) and can no longer return to flesh (there is no living anchor to re-clothe them). Creation is jammed in the threshold: **nothing can die, nothing can truly live.** The **Blight** is the *leak* — the ceaseless pressure of millions of trapped dead straining against a binding that will neither release nor renew them, venting into matter. The **Long Dusk** is the world's single death-throe stretched to forever.

**Why the sun went out.** The sun was the world's natural door — the passage souls took when they died (the psychopomp; the threshold itself). To *keep* souls and achieve immortality, the Communion deliberately **severed that door**. With no souls to carry and no role left, the sun guttered out into the earth — sinking, fittingly, at the exact spot where the rite was sung (the capital). *(This is the in-fiction ground survival-systems endorsed: the sun WAS the world's natural purge; Hearth-light is a salvaged, rekindled fragment of it; the Long Dusk clock exists because the natural sink is gone and corruption now rises with nowhere to drain.)*

**Can it be allowed to die?** Yes — but only by *reopening the door the king closed.* That requires something the binding cannot fully hold and cannot fully consume: a soul standing in the threshold. That is the Revenant.

**What you are.** A **Revenant** is a *threshold-soul* [proposed canon] — a soul the Communion's net snagged at the instant of binding but could not bind, because you were already half-through the dying sun's door when it slammed shut. You are caught in the gap: neither released into death nor woven into the web. That gap is your power and your curse:
- You can **walk in the rot** because the binding cannot grip you cleanly — you are unbound dead, not its property.
- You can **bend it** because you carry a fragment of the severed door itself: an ember of the guttered sun, the **deathlight** [proposed canon]. It is why you alone can kindle Hearths (relighting shards of the dead sun), why corruption can be purged *through* you, and why only a Revenant can stand before the Hollow Crown and act on the anchor.
- You are, in essence, **the world's aborted death, walking** — a piece of the door the king closed, looking for the lock.

The three endings (§10) are the three things a fragment-of-the-door can do at the centre: **open it** (End it / the Pyre), **become the new anchor and keep it shut** (Master it / the Crown), or **dissolve into the web** (Be consumed / the Hollowing).

## 2. Deep Lore — the Witherfall & the Communion

**The living kingdom — the Reach** [proposed canon: pre-fall name; the rotted present is the canonical **Witherreach**].
A late-sun civilization built in a vast basin — a great caldera-valley — around its capital at the lowest, central point. It was an old kingdom under an old, visibly waning sun (dying-earth register, per references §6: Wolfe). Its people already lived in a slow, natural twilight, which is precisely why death felt like an approaching tide and immortality felt like the only answer.

**The Sovereign Vael, the Ever-King** [proposed canon — the god-king's living identity; becomes the **Hollow Crown** (LOCKED) after the fall].
The last king of the Reach. Ancient, sun-crowned, terrified of the dark coming for the world and for him. He commissioned the Communion not from malice but from grief and fear — the most human motive, scaled to a god's power. This matters tonally: the architect of the apocalypse was *afraid*, not evil. Grimdark, not nihilistic (§6).

**The Choir** [proposed canon — the order that performed the rite].
Priest-singers who designed and *sang* the Communion into the land. The rite is sonic — bound through song (this grounds the audio direction §6: funeral folk, ritual percussion, "the dark's breath," and the Choister/Drowned-Choir content below). The Choir's surviving, mind-dissolved remnants still sing the unending rite — they are the **Choristers** (Wake Afflicter tier, §7) and the **Drowned Choir** Warden (§8).

**The Crowned Circle** [proposed canon — the king's inner circle].
The high nobles and officiants who underwent the Communion first and most deeply, woven closest to the anchor. When the web locked, each met a different fate by the choice they'd made in life — to rule, to feed, to atone. These are the **named, late/apex Wardens** (§8): the Famished King (chose to feed), the Ashen Penitent (chose to atone — and founded the resistance before her corruption), and others. The Circle personalizes the catastrophe: the bosses are the people who *did this*.

**The Communion (the Witherfall) — the moment.** The rite was sung across the Reach. It bound everything. It held for a breath. The anchor died. The web locked. The sun guttered into the earth. The Long Dusk fell. This single event is the **Witherfall** (LOCKED).

## 3. Timeline (eras, not dates — dying-earth register avoids precise chronology)

1. **The Long Noon** *(the age before)* — the Reach at its weary height under a waning sun. Death feared as an incoming tide. The Sovereign Vael, old and afraid.
2. **The Sun's Bargain** — the Choir devises the Communion: bind all souls, sever the sun's door, win shared immortality. The Crowned Circle undergo it first.
3. **The Witherfall** *(the Communion)* — the rite is sung; it holds for a breath; the anchor (the king) dies; the web locks; the sun guts into the earth; the Long Dusk falls. **Time zero.**
4. **The First Dusk / the Drowning** — immediate aftermath. The dead don't pass; the living can't be reborn; the Blight begins leaking. Society collapses. The first Wake rise (minds that dissolved fastest). The Choir is scattered and drowned in the rising rot. The **Ashen Wardens** are founded by those who refused/repented the rite (led by the figure who becomes the First Warden / Ashen Penitent), salvaging sun-embers into the first Hearths and the lightcraft tradition. Soon after, the **Communed** coalesce: those who believe the rite was not wrong but *unfinished*, and choose to embrace the Blight.
5. **The Long Dusk** *(generations later — the present)* — the Reach is the Witherreach. Survivors cling to salvaged Hearth-light. The factions are entrenched. The Blight tide creeps up the basin; the **Tides** deepen (LOCKED escalation clock). The Hollow Crown sits unmoving at the heart.
6. **The Thinning** *(the player's "now")* [proposed canon — the urgency hook] — after generations, the locked binding is finally *degrading*. Its grip is slipping, which is exactly why **threshold-souls — Revenants — are now waking loose** across the rim. The world nears a tipping point: total collapse into Wake, or a last window to act on the anchor. **This is why the player exists now and why the clock has teeth.**

## 4. What the Revenant Is — and how it grounds the mechanics

You are a threshold-soul carrying a deathlight ember (§1). This is not flavor — it is the in-fiction reason the corruption economy exists *on you*:

| Mechanic (LOCKED) | Fiction (this brief) |
|---|---|
| **Taint** (carried corruption, spendable + threatening) | Trapped-soul-pressure you've taken *into* yourself by surviving the rot (blighted food, the dark, Wake essence). It is the web's substance, internalized. |
| **Spending Taint** (rot-magic, tempering, ascension) | Venting the internalized rot back out as force. *("Seam B," locked with rpg-combat: you can only spend what you've already taken in; a rot-mage is a vessel saturated with rot who expels it — never channeling live through the body in the moment.)* |
| **Purging at a Hearth** | Feeding your loose excess corruption into the Hearth's fragment of sun-door, which **sequesters** it. Paid for in clean fuel/materials (rekindling the shard costs). Cannot drop you below your build's **floor** — the corruption woven into your path/skills/tempered gear is part of you now, not loose excess. *(Matches survival-systems' three constraints exactly.)* |
| **Hollowing** (permanent track; turn at max) | Your own soul being slowly overwritten and drawn into the web. Each death and each overflow ratchets you further in. At maximum, the web claims you — you **turn**. |
| **Greater Hearths roll back decay** | A Greater Hearth is a large sun-door fragment — a regional **corruption sink** that holds the local trapped-soul-pressure, pushing the decay state back a step. Let it go unfueled and it can no longer hold what it's taken; it goes dark and the held rot floods back (decay creeps in). |
| **Light suppresses Taint / dark accelerates it** | Light is presence of the cleansing the sun used to provide for free; dark is its absence. "Earn the Light" = manually doing what the dead sun once did. |
| **The Long Dusk / Tides clock** | The web straining harder as the binding degrades; more minds dissolve into Wake; the leak worsens; the basin drowns for lack of the lost door. |

**The Hollowing as a fiction — 5 visible stages over 10 mechanical pips** (matches survival-systems exactly; write marks to these):
- **Stage 1 — the Marking** *(pips 0–3):* cosmetic marks (ash-veined skin, dimming eyes), faint whispers (the web's trapped voices begin to reach you).
- **Stage 2 — the Souring** *(pips 4–6):* Warded ways weaken, Tainted ways strengthen; the Wake grow *less* aggressive toward you — fictionally, **the web begins to recognize you as its own** ("you smell like them").
- **Stage 3 — the Pull** *(pips 7–8):* involuntary twitches, vision corruption, living NPCs recoil; you are being reeled into the web.
- **Stage 4 — the Brink** *(pip 9):* strong audiovisual telegraph; last-chance rites unlock (a Warded **Last Rite** cleansing or a Communed **vigil**); co-op party explicitly warned.
- **Stage 5 — the Turning** *(pip 10):* your soul joins the web; you become a named Wake-creature (§7, §10-ending-3). **Always telegraphed; never a surprise.**

## 5. The Faction Triangle

Three poles. Two are factions of the living with opposed answers to the same question (*resist the rot, or master it?*); the third is not a faction at all but the tide both are losing to. The player's **build / Taint-floor** is the physical expression of which pole they drift toward — the fiction makes the systems' Warded/Tainted identity *legible*.

### 5a. The Ashen Wardens — keepers of the light *(allied to the **Warded** path; LOCKED faction)*
- **Belief:** the Communion was a sin against death itself. Salvation is to **resist** corruption, hold the light, honor the old human ways (names, faces, burial), and — for the most devout — find the way to let the world die *cleanly*. They cremate their dead (a defiant little mimicry of the lost sun-door — a tiny, willful release; ash is sacred — hence **Ashen**).
- **Lightcraft** [proposed canon, locked with rpg-combat]: the Wardens' magic — blessed oil and hearth-fire weapon enchants, protective auras, a mobile mini-Hearth ("Warden's Aegis"), field-purge. Powered by **clean resources and hearth-fire, never Taint.** *(Warded is NOT purely martial: two magics with opposite fuels — Lightcraft burns the light, rot-magic burns the dark.)*
- **Internal fracture (quest fuel):** *Hearth-keepers* (pragmatists — just hold the light and survive; fear that "ending it" is one more rite that will go wrong) vs *Pyre-tenders* (hardliners — the only mercy is to end the world; seek the way to unbind everything). This split *is* the End-it ending's faction politics.
- **Hook for the player:** kindle Greater Hearths, push back the Dusk, and ultimately reach the Crown to **End it**. They teach Lightcraft (Warded).
- **Leaders/figures:** Hearthmother Vesna (§6), the First Warden / Ashen Penitent (§6, §8 — their corrupted founder), the Lamplighter (§6, opening mentor).

### 5b. The Hollowing / the Communed — those who embraced the Blight *(allied to the **Tainted** path; LOCKED faction)*
- **Belief:** purity is a death-wish; the Communion was not *wrong*, it was **unfinished** — it failed only because the anchor died before it stabilized. Salvation is **mastery**: take enough rot into yourself, ascend, and either complete the rite or *seize the anchor and rule*. Mortality is the disease; the Blight is the cure for those strong enough to hold it. They embrace mutation as **transfiguration** — "we are becoming what comes after."
- **Two castes:** the **Communed** proper (lucid high-sorcerers who retain mind even at extreme Taint — aspiring god-kings) and the **Hollowing** (pilgrims feeding themselves to the Blight, sliding toward the Wake in hope of ascension). A desperate folk-horror cult quality (references §6: *The VVitch*).
- **Black Hearth** [proposed canon]: their anti-Hearth — a sink run in reverse, that *feeds on* offered Taint rather than purging it, deepening rather than holding. Their settlements cluster around one.
- **Hook for the player:** embrace the Tainted path, master the Blight, reach the Crown to **Master it**. Tension worth playing: do they want to *crown you* — or use you as the key and seat their own prophet on the anchor?
- **Leader/figures:** the Hollow Heir, Lysandra Vael (§6), the Pale Cantor (§6 — a lucid defector who knows the song), the Famished King (§6, §8 — the Circle-member who chose to feed).

### 5c. The Wake — the rising tide *(LOCKED; NOT a faction with intent — see §7)*
The bound dead whose minds have fully dissolved into the web's hunger. Not evil — **grief made predatory**: each Wake is a trapped soul clawing to re-clothe itself in living flesh, hunting the warm and the unbound because that is the closest thing to the vessel the Communion promised and can never deliver. They cannot be reasoned with; the mind is gone, only the binding's hunger remains. Their pressure scales with the player's **Taint** (a Taint-rich Revenant is the richest possible vessel) and with the **Tide** (LOCKED). Full bestiary in §7.

**The triangle in play:** the living poles (Wardens vs Communed) court the player for opposite ends of the same journey to the Crown; the Wake is the clock both are losing to and the fate the player personally races (Hollowing → turning). Every faction interaction is also a referendum on the player's drift.

## 6. NPC Roster

*(Names [proposed canon]; functions and relationships are load-bearing.)*

**Opening / mentor**
- **The Lamplighter** — an old Ashen Warden who finds the player's unburied body at the rim and tells them what they are. The tutorial voice and the game's moral anchor; carries an old salvaged lamp (a deathlight relic). Grieving, kind, clear-eyed. Survives into the mid-game as the player's conscience; his fate can vary by player choices.

**Ashen Wardens (Warded / End-it line)**
- **Hearthmother Vesna** — leader of **Ashfast** [proposed canon], the great Hearth-hold that is the player's first hub (R1). Old, principled, politically besieged: holding the Hearth-keeper line while the Pyre-tenders push to end the world. Primary Warded quest-giver.
- **Wren** — a young Warden the player can mentor; embodies hope and the Warded ideal. Co-op-flavor companion / recurring questline NPC. Her survival is a barometer of the Warded path.
- **The First Warden / the Ashen Penitent** — the legendary founder of the Wardens (a Crowned-Circle officiant who *repented*), who walked to the Crown generations ago to end the world and **failed**, becoming the corrupted cathedral-Warden (R5, §8). Met first through relics and echoes, then fought. Her failure teaches the End-it line's central lesson: a living human could not be the door — **only a Revenant can.**

**The Communed (Tainted / Master-it line)**
- **The Hollow Heir, Lysandra Vael** — the lucid prophet of the Communed; claims soul-descent from the Ever-King through the web ("the Crown is an inheritance, not a throne to storm"). Carries more Taint than anyone living and keeps her mind — proof of concept for her own gospel. Seductive, serene, genuinely caring in a way that is more unsettling than cruelty. Primary Tainted quest-giver; her true aim (crown the player as her instrument, or take the anchor herself) is the line's central reveal.
- **Coll** — a Communed pilgrim, met early as a hopeful believer, who visibly slides through the Hollowing stages over the game until he turns. The soft-permadeath dread made personal; a likely "Turned Acquaintance" quest (§11).
- **The Pale Cantor** [proposed canon] — a former Choir-singer who took deep Taint but *did not fully turn* — half Chorister, half man, still partly lucid. He **remembers how the Communion was sung** and therefore how it might be *unsung* (End-it) or *re-sung with a new anchor* (Master-it). The single most important lore NPC for the mystery; a fragile, ambiguous bridge between both factions. Can be ally, betrayer, or casualty.

**The heart**
- **The Famished King** — a Crowned-Circle officiant, the king's closest, who chose to *feed* rather than rule; now an eternal hunger-thing gating the Hollow Crown (penultimate Warden, R6). Strongest named-character boss after the Crown; *feeds on the player's Taint* in combat (locked with rpg-combat). His existence reveals that each of the Circle chose a fate — and the king chose none, becoming the hollow anchor.
- **The Sovereign Vael / the Hollow Crown** (LOCKED) — the dead god-king on the throne, the anchor of the web. Not a conversational NPC until the end, where he speaks in a hollow choral voice (the Choir's massed trapped souls speaking *through* him), delivers the mystery's final truth, and the binding lays the three choices before the player.

## 7. The Wake — Bestiary Lore *(tiers locked with rpg-combat §9–§11; names + origins are mine)*

All Wake are bound dead whose minds dissolved into the web's hunger (§5c). Their *form* records what they were in life; their *behavior* is pure reclamation-hunger. Six tiers:

1. **Husks** *(Fodder — slow shambling swarm; weak to strike & light).* The ordinary dead of the Reach, minds long gone — the bulk of a bound population. Attrition and dread, not threat-per-unit. *(Name kept from rpg-combat — it's right.)*
2. **Gloamhounds & Mourncrows** *(Skirmishers — fast, flanking, punish over-extension).* The Reach's **beasts**, bound alongside the people (the Communion took *every* soul, and the old faith held that beasts have souls too). Animals' minds dissolved faster than human ones, leaving swift, sharpened predators. *(Canonizes rpg-combat's placeholder; ground-hounds + carrion-birds.)*
3. **The Gravemade** *(Brutes — heavy hyperarmor hitters)*, with the **Swollen** sub-type *(the "Bloated" — bursts into rot-gas on death, raising player Taint)*. Dead who took in the most Blight before their minds went; the body bloated and calcified, armored in fused grave-goods and hardened rot. The Swollen are Husks so saturated they rupture. *(Canonizes rpg-combat's "Bloated.")*
4. **The Choristers / the Keening Choir** *(Afflicters — ranged rot-casters; apply festering, buff swarms; priority kills).* Members of the original **Choir** — their dissolving minds still *sing the Communion*, and the unending song is now the weapon (rot-spit, festering hexes, swarm-buffs). Directly ties the Afflicter tier to the rite's machinery and to the **Drowned Choir** Warden (§8). *(Canonizes rpg-combat's "Blightspeakers.")*
5. **The Famished** *(Hunters/Stalkers — ELITE; dispatched specifically against high-Taint Revenants and in late Tides; track across a whole Expedition).* The hungriest of the bound — souls whose hunger *sharpened* instead of dulling. Functionally the web's antibodies: drawn to a Taint-saturated Revenant because it is the richest possible vessel, and to a *threshold-soul* because it is the binding's escapee. They are the web hunting to reclaim what is slipping loose — i.e., **you**. *(Name kept from rpg-combat; the literal embodiment of "corruption draws the hunt.")*
6. **The Turned** *(build-derived named elites).* When a Revenant (or NPC) reaches Hollowing max, the soul joins the web but the **form and skill persist** as a named horror — the Reach's most personal Wake. A turned Rotcaller becomes a caster-Wake (Affliction/Miasma); a turned greatweapon Ash-Knight becomes a poise-heavy brute; even a turned Warded Lantern-bearer becomes a "light-gone-wrong" anchor-Wake. Seeded into the world, build-and-Tide-scaled, and (in co-op) a hostile encounter wearing a former ally's shape. *(Fully per rpg-combat's turning model — see §10, ending 3.)*

## 9. Main Quest Arc — Acts & Beats

A literal and thematic **inward/downward spiral**: from the basin rim (least decayed, where hope clings) toward the sunken capital at the heart (terminal decay, where the choice waits). Descent = into death, into the past, into the truth.

**ACT I — THE WAKING** *(the rim; establish the loop and the stakes)*
1. **Unburied.** The Revenant wakes at the blighted rim (R1). The Lamplighter finds you, names what you are, gives you a Hearth, and tells you the truth you can't outrun: lie down and you'll *turn*. Tutorial of the corruption economy (blighted food → Taint; purge at the Hearth; light suppresses the rot).
2. **Ashfast.** Reach the first Hearth-hold; meet Hearthmother Vesna and the Wardens. The crisis: a Tide is deepening, Hearths are falling, and the Wardens need someone who can walk *deep* into the rot — a Revenant — to kindle Greater Hearths.
3. **First light.** Defeat the first Warden (the Mire-Stag) → kindle the first Greater Hearth → roll back local decay. The player feels the first taste of pushing the dark back. Relics begin hinting at the Crown and at what the player is.

**ACT II — THE DESCENT** *(inward; the factions pull; identity hardens; the mystery opens)*
4. **The other offer.** The Communed make contact (the Hollow Heir or her emissaries) with the *opposite* answer: don't resist — master. By now the player has felt the seduction of banking Taint, and their build/floor is visibly committing them. **The faction triangle activates**; both poles react to the player's drift.
5. **The rite revealed.** Through the Pale Cantor and the First Warden's relics, the player learns the Communion was a soul-binding anchored to the king that *locked* when he died, and that the sun was the severed door. They begin to grasp *why the world won't die* — and to suspect *what they are.*
6–7. **Deeper Wardens.** The Mourning Marsh (the **Drowned Choir** → Fragment of the Song) and the Hollowing Wastes (the Communed heartland, beauty-in-decay at its most seductive) — each a deeper decay state, each one chapter of the world's story.
8. **The Tide turns (Act II climax).** A scripted Long-Dusk escalation: the world visibly worsens, Hearths fall, the Famished hunt hardens. The player learns the binding is *degrading* (the Thinning, §3) — which is why threshold-souls wake now and why the Crown can at last be reached. Decision pressure spikes.

**ACT III — THE CROWN** *(the heart; the choice)*
9. **The failed mercy.** The Cathedral of Ash (R5): the corrupted **Ashen Penitent** / First Warden. Her tragedy delivers the keystone truth — a living human cannot be the door; only a Revenant can. The player now knows they are the key.
10. **The approach.** Descend into the sunken capital (R6). Both factions converge: Wardens urge End it; the Hollow Heir reveals her true aim (use you as the key, or take the anchor herself). The **Famished King** bars the throne — and reveals the Crowned Circle's choices.
11. **The Hollow Crown.** Sovereign Vael speaks in the Choir's massed voice, gives the mystery's last truth, and the binding lays the three choices (§10).
12. **Epilogue.** Per ending; world-state persists across sessions (LOCKED).

## 10. The Three Endings (expanded) *(frame LOCKED, bible §9; fiction mine)*

Each is a thing a fragment-of-the-door can do at the anchor. Each aligns with a path (and, per the systems, with the player's accumulated Taint/Hollowing).

### 1. End it — the Pyre *(Warded-favored; the merciful ending)*
You ascend the Crown not to take it but to **unmake the anchor**: you offer your own threshold-soul as the reopened door. The deathlight you carry flares into a true sunrise-for-one-instant; the king's binding releases; **every trapped soul — the Wake, the Communed, the dead of the Reach, and you — passes through at last.** The Long Dusk ends because the world finally, properly *dies*. The sun does not return; it simply *sets*, the way it should have generations ago. *Epilogue:* ash, silence, a dawn that is also an ending — bittersweet, the most human. Co-op: all players pass together; the world they leave is at peace because it is empty.
- **Systems tie:** cleanest for low-Hollowing / Warded players (you must be "human enough" to be a clean door). Deep-Tainted players may face a harder trial or a corrupted variant.

### 2. Master it — the Crown *(Tainted-favored; the power ending)*
You cast down the dead Sovereign and seat **your own soul as the new anchor**. The binding re-stabilizes around you — a living anchor at last. The world stops sliding toward collapse but does **not** heal: it remains the Long Dusk, now *yours*. The Wake answer to you; the Blight is your dominion; you are the new **Hollow Crown** — immortal, sovereign, alone, having become the very thing that began this. *Epilogue:* you sit the throne, the rot stills around you, the Reach has a god-king again. Power at the price of becoming the eternal prison-warden of the dead — and the seed of a next cycle (one day even you may fail, and then…). If the player refuses, the Hollow Heir takes the anchor and becomes the new tyrant.
- **Systems tie:** requires having carried/mastered enough Taint to *hold* the anchor — deep-Tainted builds. The Communed's victory.

### 3. Be consumed — the Hollowing *(the tragic/loss ending)*
Two routes: (a) reach the Crown but fail or surrender the final trial; (b) **max Hollowing anywhere in normal play** (LOCKED soft-permadeath). Your soul dissolves into the web; your mind goes; you **turn** — and because your build/path shaped you, you are seeded into the Reach as a **named elite Wake** (§7-tier-6) wearing your face and your skills. The door stays shut; the world keeps rotting; you are now part of the rot you fought. Co-op: your turned form can become a hostile encounter for your former allies. *Epilogue:* the Reach is unchanged except that it now holds one more horror — and it is you. Not a flat game-over: **your specific way of living became the specific monster that haunts the world.**
- **Systems tie:** the Hollowing track (pip 10); always telegraphed through the 5 stages and the Brink warning. The whole game is the fight against this.

## 11. Side-Quest & Environmental-Storytelling Patterns

*(Repeatable templates for section writers — generate content from these, don't one-off. Every pattern says what the systems say: light costs fuel, power costs humanity, standing still loses ground.)*

**Environmental storytelling — how place tells story:**
1. **The Tableau of the Last Moment.** Every ruin freezes the instant of the Witherfall (a family mid-meal, a market mid-trade, a deathbed where no one died). Because nothing could die, the world is a museum of an interrupted death; the player reads the catastrophe by reading frozen scenes.
2. **The Decay Gradient as narrative.** A place's decay *state* tells you how deep / how long-bound you are (§12). The further in, the more bodies and architecture are *fused* with Blight — recognizable ruins at the rim, barely-human rot-coral at the heart. Place is a timeline you walk through.
3. **The Hearth-Scar.** A dead Hearth + the bodies around it = a survivor story told in objects (who held here, what they ran out of, who they failed to save). Relighting it literally re-illuminates a lost story and reclaims the ground.
4. **The Blight-Halo.** The worst corruption pools where grief was strongest — a Communed bound a dead child to the land *here*. The richest, most dangerous Blight nodes are emotionally legible: high reward = deep grief.
5. **The Choir-Echo.** In deeper regions, surfaces still faintly carry the Communion's song (audio environmental storytelling; Choristers / Pale Cantor tie-in). The closer to the Crown, the louder the dead still sing.
6. **Relic-fragments (deathlight embers / Communion implements / journals).** Scattered collectibles that piece the mystery together across a playthrough — feed the lore, never dump it.

**Side-quest templates (each reinforces the corruption economy / the clock):**
1. **The Tended Flame** *(Warded-flavored).* Hold or relight a failing Hearth/settlement against an encroaching Tide. Tests "Earn the Light." Reward: a forward Hearth, a Warded ally. **Failure-state:** the settlement falls if you're too slow — the clock has teeth.
2. **The Last Wish** *(mercy/moral miniature).* A lucid half-turned NPC wants release. Grant mercy (a clean cremation — costs scarce clean resources, foreshadows the Pyre) or harvest them for Blight (costs humanity, foreshadows the Crown). The corruption economy as a one-scene moral choice.
3. **The Ascension Bargain** *(Tainted-flavored).* A Communed offers a Taint-fueled upgrade in exchange for something corrupting (feed a relic to a Blight-node; sacrifice a clean cache). Tests the bank-vs-purge seduction.
4. **The Turned Acquaintance.** An NPC you knew (e.g., Coll) reappears having turned; you must put them down — or, rarely, find a way to save them. The soft-permadeath dread made personal.
5. **The Reliquary Delve.** A landmark dungeon (a **Reliquary**, LOCKED) holding a Communion-era secret + a build-defining reward. Each = one concentrated dose of the central mystery; risk scales with depth/decay.
6. **The Encroachment.** A timed defense: a Tide is reclaiming held ground; shore up Hearths or evacuate. Embodies "the world is already dying" / "die forward."

The Reach built its capital at the lowest, central point, and that is where the sun guttered into the earth — so the **Blight bleeds outward from the Crown at the centre; decay is worst at the heart and thins toward the rim.** The player starts at the rim and pushes **inward and downward** toward the Crown. ## 14. Visual & Audio Canon *(locked with writer-presentation for GDD §11/§14/§15; derived from the fiction above)*

**Master palette axis (THE rule):** two opposed light-poles. **Warm pale-gold = release** (the sun / the door / the Hearth / the Warded / mercy). **Cold iridescent spectral = capture** (the Blight / the web / the trapped souls / the Tainted). Every space, faction, meter, and ending reads on this axis; the heart (R6) is where the two poles collide.

1. **Deathlight & Hearth-light** — cold *salvaged-sun* ember: pale gold→bone-white, low and tired (a fragment of the *waning* late sun, **not fire** — never campfire-orange), the only relatively-warm light against the Blight; liminal dawn/dusk threshold quality (it is the door-light). **Hierarchy:** carried ember (faint, guttering, personal) < Hearth (steady pale-gold pool) < Greater Hearth (a small false dawn over a region). **Hollowing telegraph:** the carried deathlight *dims* as the Revenant Hollows (the ember guttering as the soul is drawn into the web).
2. **Blight color** — iridescent rot, more luminous/beautiful as it deepens (mirrors the Tainted seduction). It is **spectral soul-glow** (ghostlight, will-o'-wisp, funereal) — NOT chemical/radioactive; it is leaking trapped souls, keep it haunting, not sci-fi. **Decay-state ramp:** Lingering (grey, near-untinted) → Festering (first violet bruising) → Withering (deep purples, fusion) → Blooming (full iridescent bloom + teal soul-glow = the Shimmer peak, most beautiful) → Terminal (oversaturated, near-blinding spectral light). Luminosity/saturation rises inward.
3. **Wardens** — SHARED signature + DISTINCT classes. Shared: every Warden is grown around a blazing **core of concentrated trapped-soul-light** (the apex node = combat weak-point, e.g. the Penitent's clean-only core). Classes read distinct: **beast-Wardens** (Mire-Stag, Cinder-Alpha) = the Blight wearing a mutated ANIMAL ("nature corrupted," no human regalia); **named Crowned-Circle Wardens** (Ashen Penitent, Famished King) = the Blight wearing a PERSON who *chose* this — retain human/regal/ceremonial iconography (Communion vestments, the Choir's regalia, feast/funeral trappings), fused into monuments to their choice. Named-tier sub-motif: fragments of the sun-regalia / Crown mark them as "of the Crown."
4. **The Hollow Crown / Sunhold (R6, Terminal)** — "where the sun guttered into the earth" = a sunken caldera-floor where the dead sun is **buried**, leaking a sick parody of dawnlight *upward from below the ground* (a false sunrise from a grave), corrupted by the soul-pressure pooled there. The throne-city is fused into a single rot-organism (the city IS a body). **Sovereign Vael / the Hollow Crown** reads not as a standing boss but as the **load-bearing keystone of the whole web** — a withered king grown into the throne and the buried sun, threads of soul-light running *out* of him into the world; he speaks in the Choir's massed voice (many faces/voices straining at the surface of one still figure). The **Crown** itself = the sun-king's regalia, hollow/empty, a circlet of the guttered sun gone cold-grey — the literal broken door (Master-it = donning the broken door; Pyre = becoming the door it failed to be). Palette: the two poles collide — blinding cold-spectral soul-glow shot through with the buried sun's corrupted false-gold.
5. **"The dark's breath" — DIEGETIC, a literal presence (not just atmosphere)** — the unlit dark is where trapped-soul-pressure is unsuppressed (light = the lost sun's suppression). The dark audibly **breathes** — a vast slow inhale/exhale of the web (millions of held dead straining) under **whispers** (individual trapped voices — the same a Hollowing Revenant begins to hear). Intensity scales with darkness AND the player's Taint (high Taint = the web recognizing you) → a readable diegetic Taint/dark telegraph. **Second related layer:** the **Choir-Echo** — the structured *song* of the Communion itself, localized to Choristers, loudest near the Crown (vs the breath = unstructured ambient web). As a Revenant Hollows, the whispers resolve from ambient → directed (Stage 3 "the Pull") — diegetic Hollowing escalation.
