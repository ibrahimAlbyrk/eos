## 0. Bottom line up front (the decisions)

- **Closest comps:** Enshrouded (mechanical/tonal twin, 3M+ in EA), V Rising (dark survival-ARPG fusion, 6M), Valheim (co-op survival-craft ceiling, 17M). Premium dark-fantasy ceiling set by Lies of P (4M) / Elden Ring (30M).
- **Price:** **$29.99 at Early-Access launch**, rising to **$34.99 at 1.0**. (V Rising's exact, proven ladder; above Valheim's $20 because our 3D weighty combat + RPG depth cost more to build and signal more value.)
- **Model:** **Premium one-time purchase. No F2P, no pay-to-win, no DLC during EA.** Post-1.0: optional **cosmetic-only** monetization (Hearth/armor/Wake skins) + one **paid narrative expansion** ~12–18 months after 1.0. Free content updates throughout.
- **Early Access:** **~18–24 months**, 4–5 major content drops (Tides/regions cadence ~quarterly). EA is the funding and tuning engine for the keystone corruption economy — not optional.
- **Scope/team/timeline:** **core team 18–28**, **$3–6M [EST]** to EA, **~3–4 yrs to EA + ~2 yrs in EA** (~5–6 yrs total). This is the V Rising / Enshrouded production class, not the 5-person Valheim miracle (do not plan for the outlier).
- **Realistic target (SOM):** EA-year floor **300–500k units**; base case **1–3M lifetime**; breakout **5M+**. Break-even is comfortably inside the floor at $29.99.
- **Accessibility:** difficulty/assist options are now **table-stakes, not optional**. Ship Soulslike default + a non-stigmatized assist layer at launch.

**Reading the table — what actually drove success vs failure:**

1. **The $20–30 survival-co-op band is the proven sweet spot.** Every survival breakout (Valheim, V Rising, Enshrouded, Zomboid) launched at **$15–30**, not $40–60. Low entry price + co-op = friends buy in packs of 2–4 (our exact co-op unit). Premium Soulslikes sit at $60 but sell on single-player polish, not viral group buy-in. WITHERREACH is a survival-co-op first → price in the survival band, not the Soulslike band.
2. **Early Access is the dominant launch path for this genre and it works.** Valheim, V Rising, Enshrouded, Conan, Zomboid all used EA. In 2024, **28% of Steam's top-grossing new releases were EA titles** despite EA being ~14% of releases — EA over-indexes among winners. EA funds the build *and* de-risks the hardest design problem (our keystone corruption economy needs live tuning).
3. **Co-op + streamability is the growth multiplier.** Enshrouded (160k peak), Valheim (502k peak), Zomboid's Build41 co-op spike — the concurrent-player explosions all rode co-op + Twitch/YouTube. Our 2–4 co-op with built-in role structure (Warded anchor / Tainted strikers / Blight-transfer revive) is natively streamable interdependence.
4. **Failure mode = scope creep and budget mismatch.** Lords of the Fallen burned **$66M** for ~2M sales (~breakeven-ish for AAA). Survival-crafting is explicitly flagged as a genre "where costs can easily exceed revenue potential due to scope creep." Our defense: ship a tight vertical slice of the one-meter economy, expand in EA, never gold-plate.
5. **Free updates build the long tail; monetization comes later and stays cosmetic.** DST and Valheim sustained years on free updates. Conan only added monetization (Battle Pass / cosmetic Bazaar) *post-launch* and kept it strictly **non-power** (recipes, not items). That is the safe template.

## 2. Target audience definition & sizing

**Primary persona — "The Hardcore Survivalist-RPG fan" (per bible §12):**
PC/console players, **age 18–35 core**, who own *both* deep survival-craft (Valheim, V Rising) *and* RPG/Soulslike titles (Elden Ring, Lies of P). Comfortable with difficulty, scarcity, permadeath-adjacent stakes. Plays in a **2–4 friend co-op unit** (the buy-multiplier). Steam-first, controller-friendly.

**Secondary:** solo dark-fantasy RPG/Soulslike fans who want a persistent world (the Elden-Ring-curious who don't normally touch survival), and the survival-streamer/content ecosystem.

**Sizing (top-down, real overlap):**
- **TAM (genre appetite):** survival-craft + dark-fantasy-ARPG audiences each measured in the tens of millions — Valheim 17M, Zomboid ~15M, DST ~9.5M, V Rising 6M; dark-fantasy ceiling Elden Ring 30M, Dark Souls trilogy ~35M combined. The *intersection* (people who own a survival-craft AND a Soulslike) is the bullseye and is plausibly **8–15M [EST]** addressable.
- **SAM (realistically reachable in 3 yrs):** the directly-comparable EA-survival winners cluster at **3–6M** (Enshrouded 3M, V Rising 6M). That is the realistic addressable band for a well-executed, well-marketed entrant with a genuine USP.
- **SOM (our decided targets):**
  - **Floor / break-even:** **300–500k units** in EA Year 1. At $29.99 net (~$18–20 after Steam's 30% + VAT/refunds) this is **~$6–10M net** — covers the $3–6M build with margin.
  - **Base case:** **1–3M lifetime** over 2–3 years (the Enshrouded trajectory). Net **~$25–60M**.
  - **Breakout:** **5M+** (V Rising / lower-Valheim tier). Requires a viral co-op moment + sustained free updates.

**Why this audience is reachable now:** the corruption-as-currency USP is the one thing none of the comps do cleanly (bible §4). The audience has demonstrably bought *every adjacent half* of our pitch — we are selling them the fusion they keep buying piecemeal.

## 3. Pricing strategy & Steam Early-Access plan (DECIDED)

**Price ladder (copying V Rising's proven exact ladder, anchored one tier above Valheim):**
- **EA launch: $29.99.** Matches Enshrouded (our twin) and the upper survival band. Above Valheim's $20 because 3D weighty Soulslike combat + RPG build depth + narrative arc cost materially more to build and justify the value signal.
- **1.0 launch: $34.99.** The V Rising move — raise at full release to capture value and reward early adopters. Announce the hike weeks ahead (V Rising did; it converts fence-sitters).
- **No launch discount below $29.99.** First seasonal sale no earlier than ~3 months post-EA, ≤15%.
- **Regional pricing + co-op-friendly:** no official multi-pack needed (Steam gifting covers the 2–4 unit), but keep the price low enough that a 4-friend buy-in is an easy ~$120 group decision.

**Early-Access plan:**
- **Duration: 18–24 months.** Long enough to tune the keystone corruption economy with real telemetry; short enough to avoid Zomboid-style perpetual-EA fatigue (acceptable but not our goal). Faster than Valheim's 5.5 yr outlier; on par with V Rising's 2 yr and Conan's 1.5 yr.
- **EA scope at launch (the vertical slice that must be true):** the full **one-meter loop** (Taint bank/purge at the Hearth), 1 weighty combat triangle (melee/ranged/rot-magic), **2–3 regions** with decay states, 1–2 **Wardens**, both **Warded/Tainted** path skeletons, **2–4 co-op** with Blight-transfer revive, soft-permadeath/Hollowing. The corruption economy must feel complete on day one even if content is thin — the USP is the meter, not the map.
- **Content cadence: ~quarterly major drops, 4–5 across EA**, themed as **Tides** (canonical: deepening Long Dusk eras) — a built-in narrative wrapper for content patches. Each Tide adds a region + Warden + path skills + Wake variants.
- **EA → 1.0 graduation = the Hollow Crown endgame + all regions + the three endings.** 1.0 is when the *story can be finished* (bible §9).

## 4. Monetization stance (DECIDED — one recommendation, with rationale)

**Decision: Premium one-time purchase, full stop, for launch. Cosmetic-only monetization after 1.0. One paid narrative expansion.**

Rationale, tied to the comps and the design:
- **F2P is wrong for this game.** F2P demands retention-grind loops and aggressive monetization hooks that fight our melancholic, finite, narrative-driven design. None of our successful comps are F2P. Reject.
- **Pay-to-win is doubly forbidden here.** Our core resource (Taint) *is* both power and survival risk (bible §8). Selling power or safety would collapse the keystone tension. Conan's model is the lesson: monetization is **recipes and cosmetics, never items/stats**.
- **No DLC during EA.** Players paying to beta-test should never be asked to also buy content mid-EA. All EA content is free (matches Valheim/Enshrouded/V Rising).
- **Post-1.0 cosmetic layer (optional, non-power):** Hearth skins, armor/weapon transmogs, Wake/Revenant cosmetic variants, ritual emotes. Dark-fantasy cosmetics (the seduction of corruption made visible) fit the fantasy *thematically* — a Tainted player *wants* to show their warping. This is Conan's Bazaar template, kept strictly cosmetic.
- **One paid narrative expansion ~12–18 months post-1.0:** a new region + Warden + a slice of the Hollow Crown mythos, $14.99–$19.99 (Elden Ring's Shadow of the Erdtree proved expansions extend the tail and re-spike sales). Free updates continue alongside (DST/Valheim long-tail model).

Net: revenue = strong premium launch + sustained free-update goodwill + modest cosmetic trickle + one expansion spike. Predictable, on-brand, low-risk.

## 5. Production scope, team & timeline (DECIDED — indie-to-mid reality)

**Do not plan for the Valheim outlier (5 people).** A 3D, weighty-combat, RPG-deep, co-op-networked, narrative survival game is the **Enshrouded (58 staff) / V Rising (Stunlock ~30–40)** production class, not the garage miracle. Plan accordingly.

- **Core team: 18–28** through EA. Indicative split: 5–7 engineering (incl. 1–2 dedicated to co-op netcode — non-negotiable for 2–4 persistent co-op), 4–6 environment/world art (decay-state pipeline is art-heavy), 2–3 combat/animation, 3–4 systems/design (the corruption economy needs dedicated owners + a live-tuning analyst in EA), 2 narrative, 1–2 audio, 2 production/QA/community. Scale toward the upper end during EA as revenue lands (Valheim and Keen both scaled *after* the hit, not before).
- **Budget: ~$3–6M to EA [EST].** Benchmarks: a team of 8–10 runs $150–500k/yr in the cited indie data, but a 3D survival-RPG of this ambition over 3–4 years with a 20+ team lands at the low end of "mid." Lords of the Fallen's $66M is the AAA ceiling we explicitly avoid. EA revenue (floor 300–500k units ≈ $6–10M net) should **self-fund the EA period and 1.0** — the classic survival-EA flywheel.
- **Timeline:** **~3–4 years to EA launch** (concept → vertical slice → EA-ready), then **~18–24 months in EA** to 1.0 → **~5–6 years total**. Comparable to V Rising's 5 yrs of dev. Aggressive-but-real if scope is disciplined.
- **Scope discipline is the #1 business risk.** Survival-crafting is the canonical scope-creep genre. The defense is structural: the keystone is the **one-meter economy**, not breadth. Ship a narrow, deep vertical slice (few regions, one combat triangle, the full Taint loop) and let EA fund expansion. Every "more biomes / more systems" impulse during pre-EA is a budget killer — push it into Tides.
- **Console at 1.0, not EA.** Steam-first EA (per bible), console port timed to 1.0 (V Rising's playbook: PS5 at full launch). Console widens the breakout ceiling but adds cert/netcode cost — fund it from EA revenue.

## 6. Accessibility & difficulty expectations (what the market now demands)

The 2024–2025 market shifted: **difficulty/assist options are table-stakes, even in hardcore Soulslikes.**
- **Lies of P** — the gold-standard premium Soulslike — **added difficulty options** in its Overture content ("a wider audience of players could play"). Even FromSoft-adjacent purism is bending.
- **Another Crab's Treasure** shipped a granular Assist Mode (extra health, reduced damage, lower enemy HP, game-speed slowdown) that **doesn't touch story or achievements** — the now-expected pattern.
- Industry trend data points to a sharp rise in accessibility-focused titles through late 2025.

**Decision for WITHERREACH:** ship **at launch** a Soulslike-weight default *plus* a non-stigmatized assist layer — and our design makes this natural. The **Warded/Tainted path system is itself a difficulty dial**: a Warded build is the lower-floor, stabler "anchor" mode (bible §4/§8). Expose explicit assist toggles on top (Taint-gain rate, Wake hunt pressure, light-fuel drain, Blight-transfer window) so players self-select intensity without a stigmatizing "Easy" label. Keep achievements/endings intact across all settings. This widens SAM toward the Elden-Ring-curious secondary audience without diluting the hardcore core.

## 7. Key business risks (one-line each)

- **Scope creep** (genre-canonical) → discipline via vertical-slice-then-Tides; biggest budget threat.
- **USP doesn't land in playtesting** → the one-meter economy is unproven; this is *why* EA exists — tune live before 1.0 price hike.
- **Co-op netcode cost** for persistent 2–4 world → fund dedicated engineering from day one; under-resourcing this kills the buy-multiplier.
- **Crowded survival-EA field** (Enshrouded, Palworld-likes, V Rising sequels) → differentiate hard on the corruption USP and grimdark tone in every marketing beat; do not out-spend, out-distinct.
- **Perpetual-EA drift** (Zomboid path) → set the 18–24 mo 1.0 target publicly; it's acceptable but not the plan.
